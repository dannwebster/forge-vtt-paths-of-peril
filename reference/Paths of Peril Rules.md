# **Paths of Peril Rules**

# Moves

## **Nerve**

### **Dare\!**

| Row | Content |
| :---- | :---- |
| Description | When things get real and you need to act under pressure, tell the Director what you intend to do and roll \+Nerve. |
| 13+ | You do what you set out to, and either something extra happens or you do it to absolute perfection. You gain an Edge, and unmark "In Jeopardy\!" if it was marked. |
| 10-12 | You do what you set out to do. Unmark "In Jeopardy\!" if it was marked. |
| 7-9 | The Director gives you a worse outcome, a hard choice, or a price to pay. If you were In Jeopardy\!, you are still In Jeopardy\! |
| 6- | Something goes wrong. If you are not In Jeopardy\!, mark In Jeopardy\! If you are In Jeopardy\!, unmark it and take a Condition. |

### **Shoot\!**

| Row | Content |
| :---- | :---- |
| Description | When you fire from behind cover, or take aim and shoot at an enemy at range, roll \+Nerve. |
| 13+ | Do 3 Harm, or 2 Harm and gain an Edge. |
| 10-12 | Do 2 Harm **OR** do 3 Harm, but you must reposition or reload before you can take another shot. |
| 7-9 | Do 1 Harm OR 2 Harm, but you must reposition or reload before you can take another shot. |
| 6- | Your shot goes wild, or you expose yourself or another Hero. Choose yourself or another Hero. If that Hero is not In Jeopardy\!, mark In Jeopardy\! If that Hero is In Jeopardy\!, unmark it and take a Condition. |

### **Sneak\!**

| Row | Content |
| :---- | :---- |
| Description | When you try to evade detection, elude capture, disguise yourself, or otherwise sneak around, roll \+Nerve. |
| 13+ | Everyone that should fall for it falls for it, and you get a little something extra. Gain an Edge. |
| 10-12 | Everyone that should fall for it falls for it. |
| 7-9 | As above, but it's complicated. The Director chooses 1:- Someone unimportant noticed you, but that just made them important, right?- You are perceived only by a secondary sense (someone picks up your scent while you're stalking them; you're seen whispering a message but the message is not heard).- You must leave something important behind or be discovered. |
| 6- | You are noticed, but still have a chance to escape. |

## **Enemy Types**

Harm can be spread out across any number of Enemies.

| Enemy Type | 1 Harm | 2 Harm | 3 Harm | 4 Harm |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Mook | Taken Out | NA | NA | NA |
| Henchman | Disabled | Taken Out | NA | NA |
| Heavy | Scratched | Disabled | Taken Out | NA |
| Boss | Unhurt | Scratched | Disabled | Taken Out |
| Villain | Unhurt | Scratched | Disabled | Retreating |

## **Enemy Statuses**

| Status | Description |
| :---- | :---- |
| Scratched | The Enemy's defenses are down, and further hits Harm will Disable or Take them Out. Once given, the Scratched status can't be removed. |
| Disabled | The Enemy is temporarily out of the fight: pinned, injured, stunned, winded, off-balance, etc. On subsequent turns, the Enemy can take an action to remove the status, and get back into the fight, however. |
| Taken Out | The Enemy is no longer in the fight and incapable of rejoining it. Generally, this means they are unconscious, but if Harm was delivered by a weapon with the Lethal tag, then they are dead or dying. |

## **Weapon Tags**

| Tag | Description |
| :---- | :---- |
| \+X Harm | Add X to any Harm dealt. |
| Fight | Can be used with the Fight\! move. |
| Shoot | Can be used with the Shoot\! move. |
| Messy | Makes a mess that is hard to hide. |
| Loud | Can be heard from a distance. |
| Lethal | Any Enemy taken out with a Lethal weapon is presumed dead. |
| Non-Lethal | Enemies Taken Out with a Non-Lethal tag are assumed to be unconscious, for purposes of interrogation, etc. When attacking with a Weapon with both Lethal and Non-Lethal tags, the Hero must choose whether they are making a Lethal strike before rolling for the Move. |
| Utility | The weapon can also be used as a tool. You must specify what it is useful for, such as digging for a shovel or climbing for a whip. |
| Silent | Can be used to attack without making noise. A Silent attack can take out an Enemy without immediately alerting others, unless something else gives you away. |
| Long-Range | Can be used to attack from a long way away. This allows the Hero to engage targets well beyond normal throwing or close-combat distance. |
| Reload | After you attack with this weapon, you must reload it before you can use it to attack again. |
| Unreliable | The weapon is prone to jamming, breaking, misfiring, or otherwise failing at the worst possible moment. |

## **Conditions**

### **Taking Conditions**

Some bad rolls cause a Hero to take a Condition.

The first Condition is **In Jeopardy\!**. If **In Jeopardy\!** is not marked, mark it instead of taking another Condition. If **In Jeopardy\!** is already marked, take a Condition. Conditions other than In Jeopardy are called Durable Conditions.

If a Move lets you mark **In Jeopardy\!** as an optional consequence to deal more Harm or increase the effect, you can only choose that option if **In Jeopardy\!** is not already marked. You cannot choose it if doing so would cause you to take a lasting Condition instead.

Beyond **In Jeopardy\!**, there are three Condition boxes: **\-0**, **\-1**, and **\-2**. When you take a Condition, mark the next open box in order, from **\-0** to **\-2**, and write a short description of the injury or setback that caused it.

A **\-0** Condition should be minor: *shaken, winded, scratched*, and so on. A **\-2** Condition should be severe: *gut-shot, brutalized, shell-shocked*, and so on.

While you have one or more marked Conditions, you take the penalty from the highest marked Condition. These penalties are **not cumulative**.

If all your Condition boxes are marked and you would take another Condition, you die.

### **Clearing Conditions**

#### **Clearing In Jeopardy\!**

During a Peril such as a Fight, Chase, or Trap, In Jeopardy\! can be cleared by rolling 10+ on Dare\! or by burning an Edge. In Jeopardy\! is always cleared automatically at the end of a Peril.

#### **Clearing Other Conditions**

To clear a Condition other than **In Jeopardy\!**, a Hero must have downtime and share their **Virtue** or **Vice** with another Hero. The two Heroes roleplay a brief scene in which that Virtue or Vice comes into play. At the end of the scene, each participating Hero may clear a Condition.

Conditions are always cleared from least severe to most severe, starting with **\-0** and ending with **\-2**.

## **Gaining an Edge**

Some moves allow you to gain an Edge. You can only have 1 Edge at a time, but you can give your Edge to another Hero. If you gain an additional Edge, you must either lose it, or hand it off to another Hero. An **Edge** gives **\+1 in the scene until you burn it.** This is represented by a token the Director gives you. An Edge represents momentum in the fight, the high ground, or some other advantage over the situation or your foes.

At any time, you can burn an Edge for one of the following benefits:

* Remove the **In Jeopardy\!** Condition from yourself or another Hero.  
* Improve a roll by 1 Rank (e.g., from 6- to 7-9, from 7-9 to 10-12, or from 10-12 to 13+).

Since you can use an Edge you have gained to benefit another Hero, you can effectively Help Out by using **Fight\!** to gain an Edge, then granting that Edge to another Hero so they can clear **In Jeopardy\!** or improve a roll by 1 Rank.

## **The Shadows**

You may mark a Shadow from either Shadows of the Past or Shadows of the Uncanny to change your fate if any of the following are true:

* You fail an important roll.  
* You would take your third Condition.  
* You would otherwise die from a lethal peril.

If you are marking a Shadow to change a failed roll, treat that roll as if it were a 13+, and follow the course of action as dictated by the Move and the fiction.

If, instead, you are marking a Shadow to avoid certain death (such as a plane crash, a fall from a cliff, or a collapsing building), narrate the possible but unlikely circumstance that saved your life (such as improvising a parachute from an inflatable raft, catching a tree branch sprouting from the rock face, or slipping through the window of the collapsing building).

When you mark a Shadow of the Past, take a moment to narrate the indicated Flashback, and explain how that flashback saves your life in the present moment. Shadows of the Past may be marked in any order.

When you mark a Shadow of the Uncanny, something Weird steps in to change your fate. Narrate that strange event. The Shadow itself describes how you are permanently marked by the supernatural world. Shadows of the Uncanny are always marked in order, from the first to the last.

### **Shadows of the Past**

Narrate a flashback of:

* A mentor's lesson, and what it taught you.  
* Your fondest memory, and how it brings you strength.  
* A romantic attachment gone awry, and what you learned from it.  
* The first time you saw someone die, and how it shaped you.  
* Your darkest hour, and how you overcame it.

### **Shadows of the Uncanny**

* **Touched by Strangeness** — When you Enlighten\!, include one strange fact that is not part of real-world history. This is now true.  
* **Called down Hidden Paths** — When you indulge a Virtue or Vice to clear a Condition, the Hidden World notices. You or the Director introduces a strange sign, supernatural clue, watchful interloper, or similar intrusion.  
* **Scarred by Trackless Wastes** — Reduce your Charm by 1 and increase your Brains by 1\. Any serious conversation you have eventually bends toward the Unnatural.  
* **Bound to Forbidden Worlds** — Take the \-0 Condition "Obsessed with the Uncanny" (This overrides any existing \-0 Condition). This Condition cannot be cleared.  
* **Lost to Darkest Legend** — Retire your character from play. Their obsession has overtaken their ordinary life, and they vanish into the Weird as a rumor or a warning.

# **Chases**

A **Chase** uses a **clock** to represent the chase’s dramatic length, and a separate **Ground track** to represent whether the Adventurers are catching up, falling behind, escaping, or being cornered.

## **Core Structure**

### **1\. Set the Chase Clock**

The **Chase Clock** measures how many distinct chase beats must happen before the Chase resolves.

| Clock Size | Use For |
| :---- | :---- |
| 4 segments | Short chase: across a ballroom, down an alley, through a train car |
| 6 segments | Standard chase: city streets, jungle paths, rooftops, docks |
| 8 segments | Major set piece: across multiple locations, vehicles, hazards, factions |

Each segment is a **new challenge**.

Examples:

* Rooftop gap  
* Market crowd  
* Crumbling bridge  
* Stampeding animals  
* Police barricade  
* Swerving motorcar  
* Flooded tunnel  
* Airship rigging  
* Rival gunmen cutting across the route

---

### **2\. Track Ground Separately**

The **Ground track** goes from **\-3 to \+3**.

| Ground | Chasing | Fleeing |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| \+3 | The Heroes catch their quarry | They escape their persuers cleanly |
| \+2 | Right on their heels | Nearly free |
| \+1 | Closing in | Gaining distance |
| 0 | Even chase | Even chase |
| \-1 | Quarry pulling away | Pursuers closing |
| \-2 | Losing them | Almost caught |
| \-3 | Quarry escapes | They are caught / cornered |

## **Chase Procedure**

### **Start of Each Segment**

The Director describes the next obstacle, hazard, or complication.

Each segment should ask:

**What makes this part of the chase different from the last one?**

Then choose the most relevant Adventurer to act, or let the table decide who takes point.

---

## **Chase Move**

### **Run the Chase**

When you take the lead in a Chase segment, say how you overcome the immediate obstacle and roll with the fitting attribute.

| Attribute | Approach |
| :---- | :---- |
| Nerve | Sprint, leap, drive, dodge, or react fast |
| Brains | Outthink, navigate, predict, or improvise a route |
| Charm | Bluff, command, distract, or intimidate |
| Tough | Smash through, shove aside, or fight your way through |

### **Chase Roll Results**

| Roll | Result |
| :---- | :---- |
| 13+ | Fill in 1 Chase Clock segment, and gain 1 Ground, plus the Lead gains an Edge. |
| 10-12 | Fill in 1 Chase Clock segment and gain 1 Ground. |
| 7-9 | Fill in 1 Chase Clock segment, and the Lead chooses to either remain neutral, or gain 1 Ground and put a Hero of the Lead's choice In Jeopardy\! |
| 6- | Fill in 1 Chase Clock segment, and the Lead chooses to either lose 1 Ground, or remain neutral, but put a Hero of the Lead's choice In Jeopardy\! |

---

## **Ending the Chase**

The Chase ends immediately if the Ground track hits either extreme.

| Ground | Outcome |
| :---- | :---- |
| \+3 | Adventurers succeed: catch the quarry or escape. |
| \-3 | Adventurers fail: quarry escapes or pursuers catch them. |

If the **Chase Clock fills first**, resolve based on current Ground.

| Final Ground | Chasing Outcome | Fleeing Outcome |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| \+3 | The Heroes catch their quarry, and they are winded or otherwise disabled. One Hero gains an Edge in any subsequent encounter | The Heroes escape cleanly. One Hero gains an Edge going into any subsequent encounter |
| \+2 | The Heroes catch their quarry | The Heroes escape |
| \+1 | The Heroes catch their quarry, but one Hero is In Jeopardy\! at the beginning of the next encounter | The Heroes escape, but the pursuers have an opening to pick up their trail |
| 0 | A Standoff or new Situation | A Standoff or new Situation |
| \-1 | The Heroes lose their Quarry, but have an opening to pick up the trail | The Heroes are Caught, but one Hero gains an Edge going into any subsequent encounter |
| \-2 | The Heroes lose their quarry | The Heroes are Caught |
| \-3 | Heroes lose their quarry, and one Hero takes a Condition (even if they are not in Jeopardy) | Heroes are caught, and one Hero takes a Condition (even if they are not in Jeopardy) |

# Traps

## Creating a Trap

When the Adventurers encounter a Trap, the Director sets an Obstacle Rating. Roll **1 obstacle die** for every point of the Trap’s Obstacle Rating.

Traps have an **Obstacle Rating** from **1–7**. The rating determines how many obstacles the Adventurers must clear to overcome the Peril.

| Rating | Scale | Use For |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 3 | Medium | A standard meaningful trap sequence |
| 4 | Major | A dangerous set piece with multiple distinct threats |
| 5 | Chapter Ending | A climactic trap sequence that can close a major scene or chapter |
| 6 | Game Ending | A campaign-defining deathtrap, final vault, collapsing temple, or apocalyptic mechanism |
| 7 | Epic | A legendary deathtrap of overwhelming scale, complexity, or consequence |

The outcome of each obstacle die represents a specific challenge that one or more Adventurers must overcome to clear the Trap.

## **Obstacle Dice**

| Roll | Challenge Type | Description | Example |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | Hidden | After the Heroes choose the Lead, The Director chooses which applies as the Heroes encounter it | The players choose the Scholar to face the next Obstacle, the Director throws a wild jaguar into the mix, making this a \+Tough Obstacle. |
| 2 | Nerve | An obstacle that can be overcome by dexterity, precision, speed, or reflexes | Leap across a pit trap before the stones fall away or cross a chamber crawling with insects without breaking. |
| 3 | Brains | A puzzle, code, pattern, mechanism, or ancient clue that can be overcome by reasoning. | Decrypt Aztec runes to open the correct lock or recall a scrap of theology that tells you which cup *not* to drink from. |
| 4 | Charm | A secret, social clue, or personal connection is needed to solve the obstacle. | You convince a guardian to let you pass, or a flashback reveals when you got the missing phrase from an ex-lover |
| 5 | Tough | An obstacle that can be overcome by physical fortitude or violence. | Hold open a descending stone door while the others crawl through or  |
| 6 | Player’s Choice | The Lead chooses which attribute applies and describes how that kind of challenge appears. | The Daredevil in the lead takes a running jump over the chasm, because No Guts, No Glory\! |

The Director rolls the dice pool all at once, then arranges the rolled obstacles in the order that creates the best scene, rather than using the exact roll order.

## **Clearing an Obstacle**

When you attempt to clear one obstacle in a Trap, the Heroes select a Lead that will face that Obstacle of the Trap. The Director describes what the Obstacle is, and the Hero will describe how they face the challenge and roll with the matching attribute.

| Roll | Outcome |
| :---- | :---- |
| 13+ | You succeed at this obstacle and gain an Edge for the rest of the Trap. |
| 10–12 | You succeed at this obstacle and move to the next obstacle. |
| 7–9 | Either obstacle Evolves into a new obstacle (Reroll the obstacle die and pick a new challenge accordingly) OR you succeed at this obstacle, but you put a Hero in Jeopardy\! |
| 6- | This obstacle Evolves into a new obstacle. Reroll the obstacle die and pick a new challenge accordingly. Put a Hero In Jeopardy\! |

Evolving Obstacles

When a trap Evolves, the Lead rerolls the Obstacle dice, and the Director describe how the obstacle evolves into a new one that matches that new Role:

* You were sneaking past the sentry (+Nerve); but then you have to convince them you are meant to be hear (+Charm)  
* The rune lock is not just a puzzle (+Brains); solving it incorrectly awakens the guardians (+Tough).  
* The insect chamber is not just disgusting (+Nerve); the swarm drives the Adventurers toward a false exit (+Brains).  
* The descending door is not just heavy (+Tough); holding it open triggers the walls to close (+Nerve).

By default, when an Obstacle Evolves, the Lead is also the Lead for this obstacle. However, another Hero may attempt to Dare\! to become the Lead of this new Evolved obstacle.
